Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of the communication among chameleons
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Pigeons

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Camels

  • Camels are wild animals that are generally found in desert like land
  • Camels can survive 7 months in the desert without water
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat

Cicadas

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs
  • Most Cicadas have red-orange eyes
  • Their short adult life is not unusual for bugs

Pandas

  • Panda's eyes are different to normal bears
  • Panda's spend a lot of their day eating
  • Giant pandas have an extra digit on their paws that functions like a thumb and helps them to tear bamboo

Teddy Bears

  • The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip
  • Inspired by a cartoon featuring the event, shopkeeper Morris Michtom and his wife Rose made a stuffed bear with a sign 'Teddy's bear' and left it in their shop window
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906
